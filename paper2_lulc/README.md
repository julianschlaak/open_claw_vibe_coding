# Paper #2: Land-Use Change in Hydrological Modelling (mHM)

## Objective
Structured literature review on land-use and land-cover change (LULCC) in process-based hydrological models, with focus on informing future mHM-based land-use change experiments.

## Target Journal
- Hydrology and Earth System Sciences (HESS)
- Journal of Hydrology
- Water Resources Research
- Ecohydrology

## Review Structure (8 Sections)

1. **Scientific background of land-use change in hydrology**
2. **Overview of modelling studies**
3. **Hydrological processes affected by land-use change**
4. **Extreme land-use scenarios (e.g. deforestation studies)**
5. **Hydrological models used in literature**
6. **Typical hydrological responses to land-use change**
7. **Methodological approaches for scenario generation**
8. **Current research gaps and opportunities for future work**

## Research Questions

1. Do studies exist simulating LULC change in hydrological/soil water balance models?
2. How does LULC affect individual processes (interception, ET, infiltration, runoff, baseflow, recharge)?
3. Extreme scenarios: deforestation, forest-to-agriculture, afforestation, urbanization
4. Models used: SWAT, VIC, HBV, MIKE SHE, WRF-Hydro, HEC-HMS, mHM
5. Methodological approaches: static replacement, remote sensing, CLUE-S, socio-economic coupling
6. Key findings and research gaps
