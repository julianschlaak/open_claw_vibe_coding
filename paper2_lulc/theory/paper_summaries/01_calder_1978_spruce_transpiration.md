## Context & Significance

**Historical Context:**
- Published 1978 (early period of forest hydrology)
- Part of a series of Calder papers on forest evaporation (1976, 1977, 1978)
- Precedes the widely-cited Rutter interception model (1975)
- Conducted during the Plynlimon catchment experiments (Wales, UK)

**Plynlimon Context:**
- Plynlimon experimental catchments: paired forested (Spruce) vs. grassland
- Objective: Quantify land use effect on water balance
- Instrumented catchments: Wye (grassland) vs. Severn (Spruce forest)
- Period: 1960s-1970s (long-term record)

**Significance:**
- One of the first direct transpiration measurements in spruce forest
- Comparison with evaporation model (likely Penman-Monteith or derivative)
- Informs the "forest vs. grassland" water use debate
