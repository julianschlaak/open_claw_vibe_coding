# Abstract

**Drought monitoring in Central Europe relies heavily on single-component indices that fail to capture the cascading nature of hydrological drought propagation.** We present a novel Percentile-Based Multi-Component Drought Index (MDI) that integrates soil moisture, groundwater recharge, and streamflow deficits into a unified indicator without distributional assumptions. Using the mesoscale Hydrological Model (mHM 5.13.2) at daily resolution (2005–2020), we computed MDI for five German catchments spanning the Ore Mountains to the North German Plain. The percentile-based approach compares each day to its seasonal climatology (day-of-year ranking), eliminating seasonal bias while maintaining methodological simplicity. Validation against CAMELS-DE streamflow observations yielded Kling-Gupta Efficiency (KGE) values of 0.73–0.75 for well-performing catchments. The MDI successfully captured the 2018–2020 Central European mega-drought, identifying 144 consecutive drought days in 2018 for the Chemnitz catchment. Comparison with the European Drought Impact Database (EDID) revealed a moderate correlation (r = 0.43, p = 0.09), consistent with the expected relationship between hydroclimatic drivers and societal impacts. The multi-component approach detected drought propagation from soil moisture (rapid response) through recharge (intermediate lag) to streamflow (multi-month persistence), demonstrating advantages over single-index methods. The MDI provides a robust, interpretable tool for operational drought monitoring and early warning in temperate catchments.

**Keywords:** drought index, soil moisture, hydrological modeling, mHM, Central Europe, drought propagation, percentile method
